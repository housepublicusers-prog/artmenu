Revisions should remain within the scope of the original concept. Small adjustments like color tweaks or pose refinement are allowed, but complete redesigns or significant composition changes may be treated as a new commission.


Sketch Phase:
During the sketch phase, clients may request major changes such as a new pose, composition shift, or a fresh sketch entirely. This is the ideal time for large adjustments, as the artwork is still in early development.

Line Art Phase:
Once the artwork enters the line art phase, the structure becomes locked in. At this stage, revisions are limited to minor refinements, such as adjusting proportions or small details.

Color Phase:
After moving into the color phase, only color-based revisions are allowed. The line art cannot be changed once coloring has begun.

Final Detailing & Dialogue Stage:
During the final detailing and dialogue stage, the artwork is near completion, and no additional revisions can be accommodated.
